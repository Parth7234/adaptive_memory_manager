Time-Aware LSTMs (T-LSTM / Phased LSTM)Instead of just feeding the "Time of Day" as an embedding, you need to mathematically wire the Time Delta ($\Delta t$)—the exact time elapsed since the last app usage—directly into the LSTM's core gates.How it works: You introduce a time-decay function into the Forget Gate. If $\Delta t$ is small (e.g., 5 seconds), the forget gate stays open, retaining the context because the user is clearly in a continuous flow (a "session"). If $\Delta t$ is large (e.g., 3 hours), the forget gate is forced to close, flushing the hidden state to avoid making predictions based on stale, hours-old context.The Math: You modify the standard forget gate $f_t$ with a decay function:$$ f_t = \sigma(W_f x_t + U_f h_{t-1} + b_f) \odot g(\Delta t) $$(where $g(\Delta t)$ is a monotonically decreasing function like $1 / \log(e + \Delta t)$).Edge Impact: Practically zero extra parameters. It just requires one extra calculation per inference step, but drastically reduces false positives after the phone has been locked for a while.2. Lightweight Dot-Product AttentionRight now, your LSTM compresses the entire sequence of the last 10 apps into a single 128-dimensional hidden state vector. If the most important predictor for the next app was the app opened 8 steps ago, that signal might be diluted by the time it reaches the end of the sequence.How it works: Keep your LSTM, but instead of just using the final hidden state to make the prediction, use a highly simplified Attention Mechanism. Calculate a quick dot-product score between the final hidden state and all $N$ previous hidden states. This allows the model to "look back" and assign a higher weight to specific past apps in the sequence.Edge Impact: Full multi-head Transformer attention is too heavy for your 1.5ms budget. But a simple additive or dot-product attention over a sequence of length 10 requires minimal matrix multiplications and can be easily handled by the NPU.3. Switch from LSTM to GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit)If you decide to add Attention or increase your embedding dimensions, you will need to free up compute budget to keep your ONNX model under that 83KB/1.5ms limit.How it works: A GRU does exactly what an LSTM does, but it combines the Forget and Input gates into a single "Update Gate" and merges the cell state with the hidden state.Edge Impact: This reduces the number of tensor operations inside the cell by roughly 25%. In sequence modeling for user behavior, GRUs almost always match LSTM accuracy but execute significantly faster and use less RAM. You can use the saved parameters to deepen your app embeddings.4. Hierarchical RNNs (HRNN) for Session AwarenessUser behavior is naturally hierarchical: there are "macro" patterns (what I do on Tuesday mornings) and "micro" patterns (what I do exactly 5 seconds after opening the Camera).How it works: You split the workload.Layer 1 (Session RNN): Tracks the apps opened in the current continuous session (e.g., the last 5 minutes).Layer 2 (Context RNN): Tracks the overall state of the day. The final state of Layer 1 is fed into Layer 2 when a session ends.Edge Impact: This is slightly more complex to manage in an OS background service, as you need to explicitly define what a "session boundary" is (e.g., phone screen locked for > 2 minutes). However, it prevents the model from cross-contaminating contexts (e.g., bleeding your "morning workout" app sequence into your "arriving at the office" app sequence).
